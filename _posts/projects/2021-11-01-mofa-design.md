A visual communication and design project completed during my internship at Taiwan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA), Department of North American Affairs. My work spanned diplomatic briefing decks and official branding assets used in high-level Taiwan–U.S. engagements.

## Approach

Working within a government institution, I treated every assigned task as a chance to get the details right. To me, design here was never just about aesthetics—it was about making information clearer and shaping the first impression of an entire department. My guiding goal was to help the Ministry move beyond a bureaucratic look while preserving the professionalism expected of an official institution. Along the way, I gained not only finished deliverables but also a working fluency in the vocabulary and communication styles needed to collaborate across departments.

## Briefing & Presentation Design

I redesigned presentation templates and briefing decks used in official settings—including a briefing for visiting West Point cadets and a Taiwan–U.S. relations talk. The redesigns focused on clearer information hierarchy and a more modern, confident visual identity, while staying appropriate for a formal diplomatic context.

## Official Mask Visual Design

I designed the key visual for official face masks worn by senior Taiwan and U.S. officials. Treating the mask as a vehicle for official messaging, I balanced creative ambition with institutional restraint—using overlapping bird wings and joined hands, alongside a simplified national emblem, to convey a strong bilateral partnership. After minor revisions to meet official requirements, the masks went into production and were worn at allied delegations and national ceremonies, including by former U.S. Secretary of State Mike Pompeo.
