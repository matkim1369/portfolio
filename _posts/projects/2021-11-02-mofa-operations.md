During my internship at Taiwan's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA), I took part in high-level meetings as a note-taker and interpreter, and helped strengthen Taiwan's diplomatic relations with key partner nations through a series of communication and outreach materials.

This work spanned both the operational and communicative sides of diplomacy—from coordinating the moving parts of official meetings to translating complex discussions in real time and shaping how they were documented and shared afterward. The experience taught me how much careful preparation and precise record-keeping underpin effective international engagement.

## What I worked on

- **Organizing community events and high-level dialogues** — coordinating logistics, agendas, and preparation for important meetings and panel sessions.
- **Liaising with visiting delegations** — facilitating exchanges and communication with international delegations hosted by the Ministry.
- **Copywriting and creative direction for owned media** — developing promotional copy and visual concepts to support the Ministry's outreach.
- **Political and economic research** — investigating regional political-economic developments and writing analytical reports for the posting unit.
